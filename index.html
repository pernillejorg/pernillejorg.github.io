Current Research

MSc Thesis – Retrieval-Augmented Models for Scientific Claim Verification (In Progress)
Pernille Bergesen, Arkaitz Zubiaga
I am currently working on my MSc thesis in Artificial Intelligence, focusing on how retrieval-augmented models behave under realistic conditions when verifying scientific claims. The project explores the interaction between information retrieval and model performance, with a particular focus on how retrieval quality, relevance, and noise affect downstream predictions. It also investigates how natural language processing techniques can be used to better filter and structure retrieved information. A key aspect of the work is understanding why such systems fail in practice, and how model confidence can be used as a signal for reliability in real-world applications. The project combines methods from natural language processing, machine learning, and information retrieval, with an emphasis on applied and data-driven analysis.
